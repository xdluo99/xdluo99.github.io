This paper investigates the optimal consumption and investment strategies for an agent whose reference point is endogenously determined. The agent is subject to a drawdown constraint on consumption, which limits the decline in consumption over time. We derive the explicit solutions for the optimal policies and characterize the effects of the reference point and drawdown constraint on the agent's decisions. Our results show that both the reference point and the drawdown constraint significantly affect the agent's consumption and investment behavior, leading to more conservative investment strategies when the drawdown constraint is binding.
